PHYLUM PORIFERA

THE SPONGES

Features:

•       Primitive metazoans: multicellular

•       Body with tissues: pinacoderm, mesohyl, choanocyte layer

•       No organs

•       No nervous system

•       No definite symmetry

•       Sessile and benthic

•       Aquatic: most marine, few fresh water spp.

LIFESTYLE OF SPONGES:

All sponges are filter feeders.

They capture microscopic food, mainly bacteria, protozoans…

BODY STRUCTURE:

Sponges have 3 body architectures: Asconoid, syconoid and leuconoid.

 

QUICK REVIEW OF ASCONOID SPONGES

In asconoid sponges, water enters through pores called ostia in the body wall, then it slowly circulates inside the atrium (a central chamber or spongocoel), where microscopic food  is captured by choanocytes. Afterward, water leaves a large opening called the osculum.

 

Quick review of SYCONOID SPONGES

The body wall is folded into numerous finger-like projections called RADIAL CANALS to increase the surface area of the flagellated/choanocyte layer. 

The choanocytes line the flagellated or radial canals, and not the atrium.

Quick review of LEUCONOID SPONGES

Water enters via incurrent canals, circulates in small rounded flagellated chambers (where food is trapped), then leaves through excurrent canal (modified atrium) then osculum.

 

Spicules of Sponges

•       Found in the mesohyl

•       May protrude and form a protective covering

•       Help resist deformation of sponges due to strong currents.
