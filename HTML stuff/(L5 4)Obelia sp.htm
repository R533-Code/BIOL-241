I.2  CLASS HYDROZOA: Obelia sp.

-  marine

- consists of hydroid colony

-  colony is made of branches called stolons with feeding polyps = gastrozooids, and reproductive polyps = gonozooids

-  the part of the gastrozooids bearing mouth and tentacles is called a hydranth.  A hydranth is enclosed in a cup-like structure called the hydrotheca.

-  each stolon is made of an internal coenosarc (epidermis, gastrodermis, and mesoglea) and an external perisarc.

     GASTROZOOIDS

•       The part of a gastrozooid bearing the mouth and the tentacles is called a hydranth.

•       The hydranth is enclosed in a casing = the hydrotheca (which is cup-like for protection).

Obelia (cont’d)

•       Gonozooids are the reproductive polyps.

•       A gonozoid contains a club-like mass of tissue = the blastostyle.

•       Buds on the blastostyle develop and detach to become free-swimming medusas.

Review of Obelia colony:

Obelia medusa

The medusa of Obelia  is free swimming and bears gonads.

It is dioecious: either with ovaries or testes.

It has:

•        16 tentacles

•       a prominent extension = the manubrium on which the mouth is located, and

•       4 radial canals.

•       no velum (no inner shelf)
